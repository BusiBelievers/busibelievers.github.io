BUSI Curriculum Standards

This standard defines learning outcomes, completion rules, and facilitator delivery expectations for Program A and Program B through the BUSI philosophy: Bonding Using Stewardship and Integrity.

Program A

Anger Management Track

Structure: 5 modules, 10 recommended instructional hours, group or individual delivery.

Core Learning Objectives

  • Identify personal escalation triggers and de-escalation techniques through self-awareness.
  • Demonstrate integrity-based accountability language during conflict reflection.
  • Apply communication that protects dignity in family, workplace, and community settings.
  • Complete repair, restitution, stewardship, and future response planning.

Completion Criteria

  • Required sessions or hours documented.
  • Integrated fillable responses completed, including trigger map, accountability statement, cooling-off plan, and 30-day response plan.
  • Facilitator confirms participant can explain the skill and demonstrate a safer response plan.
  • Pre/Post assessment completed.

Program B

Faith & Community Track

Structure: 8 core sessions, 12 to 16 recommended instructional hours, with an optional 15-topic expansion bank.

Core Learning Objectives

  • Build stewardship habits for time, finances, relationships, and opportunities.
  • Strengthen faith-based decision-making, humility, accountability, and integrity.
  • Participate in community contribution that leaves people stronger.
  • Develop a 30-day personal restoration and stewardship plan.

Completion Criteria

  • Minimum 75% attendance of planned sessions.
  • Integrated fillable responses, workbook/reflection activities, and personal stewardship plan completed.
  • Community action, referral milestone, service step, or repair commitment documented.
  • Pre/Post assessment completed.
Open Program B

Facilitator Delivery Standard

  • Every session must include bonding, objective review, instruction, guided practice, and reflection closeout.
  • Every session must produce a case note with progress and next-step commitments.
  • Facilitators should listen before judging, connect skills to stewardship, and require integrity without shame.
  • High-risk flags must be escalated to leadership within 24 hours.
  • All completion decisions require facilitator signature and date.
