Course Revenue & Sponsorship Model

This page translates the first three BUSI course recommendations into a working model: standard fees, hardship ranges, sponsored seats, participant-worker opportunities, and how income should be stewarded.

Public placement decision: publish course descriptions, standard fees, hardship access, and sponsor-seat options on the public course page. Keep allocation percentages, margin planning, staff decisions, and participant-worker pay rules on this internal page.

1. Anger Management

Public name: BUSI Anger Management & Conflict Accountability

  • Standard fee: $195
  • Hardship range: $75 to $125
  • Sponsor seat: $195
  • Recommended cohort: 8 to 12 participants
  • Best use: court/referral education when accepted, agency referrals, self-referral, employer/family accountability.

2. Responsible Choices

Public name: Responsible Choices, Anti-Theft & Restitution Stewardship

  • Standard fee: $95
  • Hardship range: $40 to $75
  • Sponsor seat: $95
  • Recommended cohort: 8 to 15 participants
  • Best use: diversion-style education, integrity, restitution, decision-making, community accountability.

3. Service + Job Readiness

Public name: Community Service Orientation & Job Readiness Track

  • Standard fee: $75
  • Hardship range: $25 to $50
  • Sponsor seat: $75
  • Recommended cohort: 10 to 20 participants
  • Best use: community service preparation, verified hours, work-readiness bridge, supervised helper pathway.

Public Sponsorship Promise

Recommended wording for public pages:

"When feasible, BUSI uses paid course income and sponsor seats to help provide hardship seats, materials, documentation, and supervised service opportunities."

Avoid promising a fixed scholarship for every paid seat unless BUSI has the cash reserve to honor it every time.

Participant-Worker Safeguards

  • No participant worker should access private participant records, payment data, passwords, or certificate approval decisions.
  • Use supervised roles: setup assistant, reminder call/text assistant using approved scripts, service crew helper, peer ambassador, cleanup helper, packet prep without private records.
  • Pay or stipend must be documented separately from court/community service hours.
  • Any helper role should require conduct standards, confidentiality notice, and supervisor sign-off.

Court and Referral Control

  • Never advertise "court-approved" without written acceptance or required licensing.
  • Save each acceptance email or letter with the BUSI reference and referral source.
  • Keep a sample certificate, attendance sheet, course outline, and facilitator credentials ready for referral review.
  • Use the requirements tracker before launching any new court-facing course.
